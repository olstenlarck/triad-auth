# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Developers building web, CLI, TV, console, and device applications. They need one small authentication broker that keeps upstream provider complexity out of every app while exposing deliberate identity semantics.

## Product Purpose

Triad brokers GitHub authentication. It gives applications a globally correlatable GitHub identity, a broker account identity, and a stable app-scoped identity through authorization-code and device flows. Success means a developer can understand the identity model in seconds and integrate without adopting a large auth platform.

## Positioning

One identity handshake, two useful IDs: global when you need continuity, pairwise when you need isolation.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: view and use the open-source GitHub repository.
- Secondary CTA: read the quickstart and inspect the live protocol endpoints.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: global identity and per-app identity can coexist.
- Belief ladder: the identifier semantics are explicit; the flows are standards-shaped; the implementation is small enough to inspect; the deployment model is simple.
- Proof on hand: the running discovery document, JWKS, source code, and test suite. No testimonials or fabricated adoption claims.

## Brand Personality

Loud, precise utility. It should feel like a machined control panel with one decisive signal color: bold enough to be remembered, sober enough to hold authentication decisions.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic gradient SaaS pages, soft rounded dashboards, glass panels, fake terminal theater, editorial-serif developer branding, tiny repeated eyebrow labels, and decorative security imagery. The Shoo reference is useful for centered composition, sharp panels, oversized type, and restrained density—not for copying its logo, copy, or exact palette.

## Design Principles

- Make identity semantics visible instead of hiding them behind “user ID.”
- Treat consent as a precise transaction: who, what, and why are always legible.
- Keep the broker inspectable; protocol artifacts are stronger proof than marketing claims.
- Use one memorable visual signal and let structure carry the rest.
- Make dangerous or irreversible actions visually unmistakable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA at minimum, retain visible keyboard focus, never encode status with color alone, keep motion optional through `prefers-reduced-motion`, and make device codes and identifiers easy to select and read.
